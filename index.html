What is Kanji Heatmap about and what is it useful for

Description 1

A tool designed to help learners of Japanese visualize and prioritize their kanji study based on frequency of use.

What it does

  1. Frequency-Based Heatmap – The site color-codes kanji based on how often they appear in written Japanese, helping learners focus on the most useful characters first.
  2. Customizable Study Focus – Users can sort and filter kanji by JLPT level, school grade (Japanese education system), frequency rank and much more
  3. Interactive Exploration – Hovering or clicking on a kanji provides details like readings, meanings, and example words.
  4. Study Planning – Helps users identify gaps in their knowledge by highlighting which high-frequency kanji they haven't learned yet.

Useful for

  • Efficiency – Prioritizes learning the kanji you’ll encounter most often.
  • Visual Learners – The heatmap format makes it easy to see importance at a glance.
  • Test Preparation – Great for JLPT or kanji kentei study by focusing on level-appropriate characters.
  • Review & Gap Analysis – Advanced learners can check if they’ve missed any common kanji.

Kanji Heatmap is particularly useful if you want a data-driven approach to kanji learning rather than just following a fixed order (like RTK or JLPT lists)
